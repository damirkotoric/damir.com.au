BrokerEngine

Ground-up redesign of a complex feature-packed web app resulting in a quantum leap forward for the mortgage brokering product, and the startup getting acquired by Australian Finance Group.

BrokerEngine is an all-in-one suite for real estate mortgage brokers. To use a tech analogy, BrokerEngine does for mortgage brokers what Atlassian's JIRA does for IT startups — it makes business processes more organised, helps businesses scale and reduces headaches for those in charge of overseeing all the work.

I came onboard a popular and mature product that grew over many years, was feature packed, and one that was in need of a holistic, systems-thinking design overhaul in order to modernise and simplify the user experience, and set the product on a course to become a game-changer in Australia's mortgage brokering industry.

{% include quotes/craig.html %}

Acquired by Australian Finance Group

During high-stakes acquisition talks between the team at BrokerEngine and Australian Finance Group, my Figma prototype was demoed in order to crystallise a vision for the future of the product. The end result? The team at AFG "loved it" and about a month later, the news was official that Australian Finance Group has acquired BrokerEngine, a milestone moment for the startup, giving it the funding and security required to execute our ambitious product vision.

One-man design orchestra

Over the course of 6 months, I was a single person design team.

My work touched on everything from grand strategy UX architecture and leadership on the strategic level — recommending which design system and Figma UI kit to choose, implementing said recommendations, introducing new design principles and UX patterns, choreographing a new core UI structure and navigation system. All the way to the minute operational-level "UX trench warfare" — patching up alpha builds of features before they are shipped, writing microcopy for error messages and picking a more appropriate icon to better convey the meaning of a button.

{% include quotes/will.html %} Complex process and BE helps all the way.

Remote User Testing

Utilising Optimal Workshop, I've set up two different tests: a tree test for the navigation structure, and a click test for the UI layout of the home page.

The turnaround time for this work was a few months — factoring in everything from project analysis, stakeholder meetings, a few iterations on the design direction, prototyping, QA and testing.
